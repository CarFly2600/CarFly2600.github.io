I decided to create a webpage interaction design based around my aviation enthusiasm by listing specific planes from different manufacturers with varying size, range, passenger capacity and safety record. The webpage will use an experiment from one of the previous assignments because it had the same basic organisation ruleset and would be the easiest to complete in the given timeframe. At the top, the user of the project will have the ability to look into specific categories to search for the ideal airliner for their next flight as a quicker alternative than scrolling down through the page for several seconds.

The Information Design principle worked best for my idea of how to implement my aviation enthusiasm into the interaction and it was the most practical out of all the given alternatives. But I also decided to give it a Playful abstract adjective to eliminate the possibility of boredom and usual old business mind setting when browsing through the webpage. This was also chosen to make it stand out among other potential sites designed for a similar purpose, and at least one of these sites have been found, but do not include as many aircraft as mine, giving more user choice.

I decided to put in little animation bits to reflect the Playful adjective combining it with the combined colours from the logos of Boeing, Airbus, McDonnell Douglas and Lockheed respectively. This also helps to subtly reflect the history of aviation in some way. Some audio has also been put in to be played for users to know the noise they would be dealing with the respective airliners the noises belong to. The page is also designed to feature only the airliners which are still in production and/or development from their respective manufacturers. Sounds were from FreeSound and copyright via Wikipedia.

The first thing users will see at the very top of the page will be a category list similar to Wikipedia, the list of categories will be vertical to stick with convention. This also allows them to act as buttons so that users can quickly filter and jump certain items on the list best suited for their purpose and avoid confusion. This is employing the Known-Item Seeking principle in UI Design because the user knows exactly what they are looking for and will be given multiple options for planes that fit certain filters depending on the users’ inputs or filters.

This interaction pair will work well together to ensure that the user is presented with all the relevant information from their filters and for the respective task it needs the information for, while also giving a positive mindset while exploring the interaction to promote good concentration leading to better choices and no potential costly mistakes leading to economic or financial problems for anyone. If this project worked well in real life, this would be an ideal choice for the aforementioned clients to make the best choices with all necessary information to fly from where they are to where they’re needed.
